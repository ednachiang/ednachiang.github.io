About Me

My name is Edna Chiang and I'm a science communicator, bioinformatician, and microbial ecologist.

I'm a 4th year PhD student in the Microbiology Doctoral Training Program at the University of Wisconsin-Madison.

I study the gut microbes living inside hibernating ground squirrels to understand how the microbe-host relationship contributes to hibernation. I'm co-advised by Dr. Garret Suen in the Department of Bacteriology and Dr. Hannah Carey in the School of Veterinary Medicine.

In addition to studying microbes, I also have PhD minors in Life Sciences Communication and Biotechnology. I'm a NSF Graduate Research Fellow, a NIH Biotechnology Training Grant Recipient, and a Wisconsin Idea STEM Fellow.

Before I started my PhD, I grew up in Michigan in the metro-Detroit suburbs. I received my B.S. from the University of Michigan in Microbiology and Spanish. I worked in Dr. Vincent Denef's lab to study freshwater microbes and understand how microbes contribute to healthy lakes.

When I'm not chasing squirrels, I like to cook, belly dance, rock climb, latin social dance (bachata is my favorite), hike, and binge Netflix.
