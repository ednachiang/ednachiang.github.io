What happens when squirrels hibernate?

To share my hibernation microbiology research with local Madison elementary-aged students, I created two outreach activities teaching hibernation physiology and hibernation microbiology.

Hibernation physiology

The first activities uses videos of squirrels waking up during hibernation and stuffed squirrels. The learning objective is for participants to identify the hibernating squirrel, which is colder in body temperature than the summer squirrel.

Hibernation microbiology

The second activity uses a spot-the-difference worksheet to highlight the differences between summer vs. hibernating squirrels. The learning objective is for participants to describe differences in the guts of hibernating squirrels compared to summer squirrels. Hibernating squirrels have less food and microes in their gut.
