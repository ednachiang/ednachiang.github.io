About Me

My name is Edna Chiang and I'm a science communicator, bioinformatician, and microbial ecologist.

I'm a 4th year PhD student in the Microbiology Doctoral Training Program at the University of Wisconsin-Madison.

I have a PhD minor in Life Sciences Communication. My training was concentrated on the science of science communiation and practical skills such as writing for expert and non-expert audiences, data analysis for social science, and web design for science (like this website that you're on!). I am also a Wisconsin Idea STEM Fellow who has been trained in interactive, informal learning and evaluating outreach activities.

In addition to public engagement, I'm also active in science policy. I'm a member of the UW-Madison group Catalysts for Science Policy as well as the National Science Policy Network. I worked as a summer Science Policy Fellow with the nation's largest biological and biomedical research advocacy group, the Federation of American Societies for Experimental Biology (FASEB).

In recognition for my commitment to research and science communication, I was awarded an NSF Graduate Research Fellowship and an NIH Biotechnology Training Grant.

Before I started my PhD, I grew up in Michigan in the metro-Detroit suburbs. I received my B.S. from the University of Michigan in Microbiology and Spanish. I worked in Dr. Vincent Denef's lab to study freshwater microbes and understand how microbes contribute to healthy lakes.
